Chapter VI · Tide and Signal
I have been meaning to ask — do you remember the place we spoke of last week?
A., eleven past two

The one past the tide pools, with the broken fencing? Yes. I was there again this morning — the gulls have come back. Whatever they were frightened of has moved on, or gone under.

He asks after the place by the tide-pools. I remember — it was on a Wednesday, the conversation about the fences. The gulls were gone then, and he seemed unsettled by their absence. Better to tell him first that they have returned, and only then name the uncertainty.

Maren, two past two
That is comforting, I think. Or perhaps not.
A., three past two

Both, probably. Most things are.
